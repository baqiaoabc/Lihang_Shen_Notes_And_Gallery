1. Given 5 numeric data points: [2, 4, 6, 8, 10], calculate the 75th percentile.
(Hint: percentile calculation method)
First, sort the data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10. The position of the 75th percentile = (5+1)*0.75 = 4.5, between the 4th and 5th data points. The 4th is 8, the 5th is 10, interpolate: 8 + 0.5*(10-8) = 9.

2. Briefly describe the difference between a Bar chart and a Histogram.
Bar chart displays categorical data with spaces between bars; each bar represents the frequency or value of a category. Histogram shows distribution of continuous numeric data with no gaps between bars; each bar represents frequency in an interval.

6. Briefly describe the frequent itemset mining process in the Apriori algorithm.
First find frequent 1-itemsets, generate candidate 2-itemsets from them, calculate support, filter frequent 2-itemsets, iterate until no new frequent itemsets are found.

Randomly select K centroids, assign data points to nearest centroid, update centroids by calculating means of assigned points, repeat until convergence.

‚úÖ Short Answer Questions - Week 10 & 11

1. What is Information Gain, and why is it used in attribute selection?
Information Gain measures how much an attribute decreases the entropy (uncertainty) in the target variable, helping to select the best attribute to split data in decision trees.
2. How do you calculate the entropy of a dataset?
Entropy is calculated as the sum of \(-p_i \log_2 p_i\) over all classes, where \(p_i\) is the proportion of instances in class i.
3. What does TF-IDF aim to represent in text data?
It reflects how important a word is to a document in a corpus by combining term frequency and inverse document frequency.
4. How is Term Frequency (TF) computed?
TF is the count of a term in a document divided by the total number of terms in that document.
5. Why do we use inverse document frequency (IDF) in TF-IDF?
IDF reduces the weight of words that appear in many documents, highlighting more unique, informative words.
6. What is the Naive Bayes assumption, and why is it called "naive"?
It assumes that all features are conditionally independent given the class label; this simplifying assumption is called "naive" because it rarely holds in practice.
7. How does Laplace smoothing work in Naive Bayes?
It adds 1 to the count of each feature to avoid zero probabilities for unseen words during classification.
8. In text classification using Naive Bayes, what are the features?
Features are typically words or terms extracted from the text documents.
9. Why is Information Gain preferred over simply counting the number of attribute values?
Because Information Gain considers the effectiveness of an attribute in reducing uncertainty about the target variable, not just its uniqueness or frequency.
10. Describe one advantage and one limitation of using Naive Bayes for text classification.
Advantage: Fast and works well with high-dimensional data. Limitation: The independence assumption may not hold, potentially reducing accuracy.
